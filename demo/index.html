KV cache is the single most important optimization for production LLM systems. But stale cache means wrong answers. Wrong answers mean angry users. And manually invalidating cache? That's a recipe for bugs and forgotten edge cases.

The Solution: Automatic Invalidation

Expanso watches your database for changes and automatically tells LMCache to clear stale entries. When your data changes, your cache updates within seconds. No custom code required.

Cache Warmup Pipeline (excerpt) ~30 lines 
input:
  generate:
    interval: 5s

pipeline:
  processors:
    - sql_raw:
        query: "SELECT id, content FROM documents
          WHERE updated_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '10s'
          AND id NOT IN (SELECT document_id
            FROM cache_warmup_log)"
    - mapping: |

          "role": "user",

    outputs:

      - sql_insert:   # Log it
          table: cache_warmup_log

FAQ

Why not just use PostgreSQL triggers?

Triggers add complexity to your database, are harder to monitor, and don't handle retries or failures gracefully. Expanso keeps your database clean and handles all the edge cases.

Why polling instead of CDC (Change Data Capture)?

CDC requires Redpanda Enterprise. For most use cases, polling every 2 seconds is plenty fast and works with the open-source version. The demo is designed to be accessible to everyone.

Does this work with other databases?

Yes! Expanso (Redpanda Connect) supports 200+ connectors including MySQL, MongoDB, S3, Kafka, and more. Just change the input configuration.

What about high-traffic production systems?

For production, you can add deduplication, batching, and rate limiting. Expanso handles millions of messages per second. This demo is simplified for clarity.
